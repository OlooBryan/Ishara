Elephants in the Maasai Mara play a vital role in maintaining the ecosystem, but conflicts with local communities have been rising. As elephants often raid crops, researchers are studying their migration paths and testing solutions like beehive fences to reduce conflict. This approach not only deters elephants but also provides income from honey, offering a balanced solution for both conservation and community needs​

Rhinos in the Maasai Mara face ongoing challenges, particularly from poaching due to high demand for their horns. Conservation efforts have helped stabilize populations, especially through anti-poaching patrols and secure habitats within protected areas. Innovative techniques, like translocating rhinos to safe zones and using dehorning as a deterrent, are also employed. By involving local communities in conservation, these efforts aim to create a balanced approach that protects rhinos while supporting local livelihoods​
